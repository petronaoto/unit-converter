Theoretical Background & Formulas

The Gas Heating Value (Compositional) calculations implemented in Version 1.8 strictly follow the principles set forth in JIS K 2301 (Fuel gases and natural gas - Methods for chemical analysis and testing), the Rackett Equation, and industry standard conversions.

1. Real vs. Ideal Gas Components

Internal state shifts between molar fraction ($x_i$) and volumetric fraction ($v_i$) are calculated dynamically using real gas compressibility ($Z_i$) at standard conditions (0°C, 1 atm):

v_i = (x_i * Z_i) / Σ(x_k * Z_k)

Heating value (HHV) is inherently tied to the pure component's higher heating value per standard unit volume. Mixed HHV is aggregated linearly by the volume fraction.

2. Wobbe Index (WI) & MCP

The Wobbe Index measures gas interchangeability regarding burner capacity, while the Maximum Combustion Potential (MCP) evaluates the flame speed based on the weighted sum of intrinsic pure component combustion rates.

WI = HHV / √(Specific Gravity)

MCP = (1 - K) * [ Σ(S_i * f_i * A_i) / Σ(f_i * A_i) ]

Where S_i is the characteristic combustion speed, f_i is the scaling factor, A_i is the combustible percentage, and K is the attenuation coefficient influenced by inert gas (N2, CO2, O2).

3. LNG Liquid Density Approximation

Rather than utilizing purely empirical static tables, this calculator leverages the analytical Rackett Equation to determine pure component molar volume at the specified cryogenic temperature, followed by ideal additive volumetric mixing:

V_i = (R * Tc / Pc) * Zc^[ 1 + (1 - Tr)^(2/7) ]

Density_mix = Σ(x_i * MW_i) / Σ(x_i * V_i)

Where Tr is the reduced temperature (T/Tc). If T > Tc (supercritical), the algorithm falls back to critical volume bounds.

Operations Manual (Version 1.8)

1. Quick Introduction

The O&G Engineering Converter is a client-side suite built for control room efficiency. It operates via multiple utility tabs:

  • General Tab: Standard unit conversions and the custom module generator.
  • Advanced Tab: Complex, iterative engineering calculators (Compositional GHV, Hydraulics, Volume, Z-Factor).
  • Theory Tab: Background formulas for validation and review.

2. Gas Heating Value & Flow Calculator (NEW)

This module implements JIS K 2301 properties to compute combustion indices and converts flow rates based on composition.

  • Fractions: You can select whether your input composition is in Molar Fraction or Volumetric Fraction using the toggle in the header.
  • Temperature & Pressure: By default, calculations assume 0°C and 0 MPaG (Normal Conditions). Modifying these will shift the volumetric flow output from Normal (Nm³) to Actual (Am³ / acf).
  • Bidirectional Flow: Mass to Volume and Volume to Mass are fully distinct blocks to prevent circular errors.
  • C6+ Components: Any heavy tails (C6 and above) must be aggregated and input as nC6 (n-Hexane) for structural approximation.

3. Operations & Quick Copy

Inputs and outputs are synchronized instantly. Use the dropdowns to adjust magnitude. Click the clipboard icon to copy any metric directly to your system clipboard.

4. Advanced: Auto-Phase Hydraulics

The Delta Pressure calculator on the Advanced tab uses a custom JavaScript iteration loop to solve the Colebrook-White friction factor. It automatically detects the flow regime based on the mass flows you provide:

  • • Single Phase Vapor: Enter only Vapor Flow (Leave Liquid at 0).
  • • Single Phase Liquid: Enter only Liquid Flow (Leave Vapor at 0).
  • • Two Phase (HEM): Enter both flows. The system calculates a mass-weighted homogeneous mixture.

5. Local Storage & Custom Modules

Click the dashed button on the General tab to build a new conversion card. Your custom modules are now saved locally to your browser. They will persist even if you close the tab.
