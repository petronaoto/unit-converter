Theoretical Background & Specifications Manual

This manual details the exact mathematical models, engineering standards, and rounding logic utilized across all blocks of the O&G Engineering Converter tool.

1. Gas Heating Value (Compositional)

Based upon the rigorous structures of JIS K 2301 and ISO 6976, taking into account specific real gas compressibilities.

1.1 Spreadsheet Harmonization (Rounding & Constants)

To maintain strict parity with customized Excel models, this specific block intercepts pure component properties defined at the 0°C combustion / 0°C metering reference state. Furthermore, to mimic Excel's cascading calculations, intermediate variable aggregations are forced through explicit Javascript rounding boundaries (`Math.round()`):

  • Specific Gravity: Calculated as the sum of pure component specific gravities ($s_i \cdot v_i$), rounded strictly to 3 decimal places (e.g., 0.618).
  • Wobbe Index: Calculated using the already rounded Specific Gravity, and subsequently rounded to 2 decimal places.
  • Maximum Combustion Potential (MCP): The attenuation factor ($K$) is evaluated and explicitly rounded to 2 decimal places (matching a typical % formatting display) before being subtracted from 1 and multiplied by the Base MCP. This is why a mathematical $35.94$ translates perfectly to a display value of 36.0.

1.2 Real vs. Ideal State Mechanics

When transitioning between molar fraction ($x_i$) and volumetric fraction ($v_i$), the equation factors in standard conditions compressibility ($Z_i$):

v_i = (x_i * Z_i) / Σ(x_k * Z_k)

1.3 Wobbe Index (WI) & MCP Formulas

Combustion interchangeability is measured through two parameters. The Wobbe Index evaluates thermal throughput capacity, while Maximum Combustion Potential (MCP) estimates specific flame speed limits influenced by attenuating inerts (N2, CO2):

WI = HHV / √(Specific Gravity)

MCP = (1 - K) * [ Σ(S_i * f_i * A_i) / Σ(f_i * A_i) ]

1.4 Cryogenic Liquid Density (Rackett Equation)

Rather than relying solely on empirical lookup tables, this module determines theoretical pure component molar volumes at a specified cryogenic LNG temperature using the Rackett Equation, followed by ideal additive mixing rules:

V_i = (R * Tc / Pc) * Zc^[ 1 + (1 - Tr)^(2/7) ]

Density_mix = Σ(x_i * MW_i) / Σ(x_i * V_i)

2. Pipe Delta Pressure Calculator

The hydraulic engine applies the Darcy-Weisbach equation combined with a dynamically iterating Colebrook-White friction factor solver. It supports single-phase and pseudo-two-phase flows.

2.1 Flow Regimes (Homogeneous Equilibrium Model)

If both Vapor and Liquid mass flows are provided, the fluid is assumed to be fully homogenized (no slip assumption). Mixture density ($\rho_{mix}$) and mixture viscosity ($\mu_{mix}$) are weighted by vapor quality ($x$):

x = W_vapor / (W_vapor + W_liquid)

1 / ρ_mix = (x / ρ_vapor) + ((1 - x) / ρ_liquid)

μ_mix = x*μ_vapor + (1 - x)*μ_liquid

3. Z-Factor Estimator (Papay's Equation)

For rapid approximation of natural gas compressibility without total composition, Papay's correlation is deployed. It calculates pseudo-critical properties ($T_{pc}$ and $P_{pc}$) directly from Specific Gravity ($Sg$), and then extracts the $Z$ factor.

P_pr = Pressure / (756.8 - 131.0*Sg - 3.6*Sg²)

T_pr = Temp_Rankine / (169.2 + 349.5*Sg - 74.0*Sg²)

Z = 1 - (3.52 * P_pr / 10^(0.9813*T_pr)) + (0.274 * P_pr² / 10^(0.8157*T_pr))
